We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled **"Multi-modal LLM for Financial Decision Intelligence: Integrating News Text and Stock Price Time-Series via Cross-Modal Attention"** for consideration in the *Journal of Financial Econometrics* Machine Learning Special Issue.

## Summary

This paper addresses a fundamental question at the intersection of machine learning and financial econometrics: does multi-modal fusion of financial news text and stock price time-series generate information content incremental to single-modality approaches? We introduce **FinMM-LLM**, a cross-modal attention architecture that aligns FinBERT-derived news embeddings with PatchTST-derived price representations through bidirectional cross-attention.

## Relevance to the Special Issue

The special issue call seeks research "strengthening the growing link between ML methodologies and economic analysis." Our contribution directly addresses this mandate on three fronts:

1. **ML Innovation**: A novel cross-modal attention architecture with gated fusion, advancing multi-modal learning for financial applications.
2. **Econometric Rigor**: Fama-French five-factor alpha estimation, Fama-MacBeth cross-sectional regressions, and placebo tests provide the identification standards expected by the financial econometrics community.
3. **Economic Mechanism**: Attention weight decomposition reveals an information diffusion channel consistent with Hong & Stein (1999), demonstrating that ML architectures can illuminate economic mechanisms — not merely optimize prediction.

## Key Results

- Annualized Sharpe ratio of 1.55 (41% improvement over best single-modality baseline)
- FF5 alpha of 72 basis points per month (t = 3.41)
- Alpha concentrated in high-information-asymmetry periods (earnings announcements, high VIX)
- Cross-modal attention intensity positively correlates with market volatility (ρ = 0.64)
